Let me take you back to when I was in 8th grade. One day, out of sheer curiosity (and maybe a little boredom), I decided to create my own website. But here’s the catch — I didn’t write a single line of code. Nope! I used Blogspot.

Back then, it felt like magic. A few clicks here, a bit of typing there, and boom — my website was live on the internet. I remember thinking, “Wait, this is it? Building a website is THIS easy?” I was shocked and honestly, pretty proud of myself.

Fast forward a few years, and the reality check hit me hard. That simple Blogspot site was just the tip of the iceberg. Real websites? They’re a whole different beast — packed with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, backend servers, databases, and so much more. Suddenly, that “easy” website I made felt more like a playground than the real deal.

That’s when I decided to dig deeper — to learn how to build websites the right way. I started with the basics: HTML and CSS. Then I tackled JavaScript, trying to bring my pages to life.

I still remember the exact moment I ran my very first custom-coded website on my browser — and it actually worked! No templates, no shortcuts. Just me and my code. That feeling? Absolutely priceless.

So, if you’re just starting and your first site is on some drag-and-drop platform — don’t worry, you’re not alone. It’s a great stepping stone. But if you dream of becoming a developer who builds things from scratch, the real adventure begins when you start coding for yourself.

And trust me, once you do, you’ll never look back.
